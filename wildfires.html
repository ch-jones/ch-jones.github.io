Wildfires

Visit Site

December 2016

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Three.js, Adobe Photoshop, Cinema4d

Prompt: Using a combination of 3D modelling and interactive code, a website was created with the intent of raising awareness surrounding global warming.

During the 2016 Fort McMurray wildfire, my extended family was uprooted, and we waited in distress to hear that they evacuated their homes safely. After knowing they were safe, we waited again, wondering if their homes and livelihoods would still be there to return to. Thankfully, my family and their homes were safe, but many were not.

Because of this situation, I became more aware of wildfires and how climate change has increased the amount and severity of them. I chose to focus my project on educating people on the direct impact climate change has on wildfires, and the forecasted effects wildfires will have on humans.

Research

At the outset, research was conducted on news articles about wildfires internationally. This was then concentrated on Canada, focusing on data on wildfires in the last 20 years. The main points from this research were that:

  • Wildfires are a natural part of forest ecosystem
  • Wildfires are relatively harmless to the earth when CO2 levels are normal and the environment is healthy.
  • Increases in CO2 levels cause wildfires to be larger, more frequent, and more dangerous
  • Wildfires are therefore harder to control, leading to unnatural environmental damage
  • They are spreading into residential areas, impacting habitats and ecosystems.
  • High CO2 levels cause hotter temperatures and drier environmental conditions globally.
  • This has started causing wildfires in ecosystems where they have never before been observed in human history, like the Arctic Tundra.
  • Wildfires will likely cause many habitats and residential areas to be uninhabitable

Wireframes

The initial concept for the interactive site was a slider so that users could slide between and see the difference between years. However, this narrative didn’t communicate the irreversible nature of climate change. To better communicate the permanent changes resulting from climate change, the UI was changed from a slider to a button, as it would move the timeline forward in fixed increments, and unable to go backward.
